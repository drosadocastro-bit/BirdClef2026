# Timeline

## Phase 1: Rapid Competition Scaffold

- adapted an existing audio stack into a BirdCLEF-oriented module
- established training, inference, and notebook paths
- built a workable project structure quickly instead of starting from zero

## Phase 2: Competition-Specific Corrections

- recognized multilabel and macro-metric implications
- identified the importance of overlap-aware soundscape handling
- documented issues around thresholding and ranking behavior

## Phase 3: CFAR Exploration

- introduced CFAR-inspired adaptive thresholding as a noise-reduction and detection idea
- treated acoustic decision logic as a signal-processing problem, not only a model-output problem
- evaluated the idea even though it did not become the intended competitive advantage

## Phase 4: Historical Consolidation

- preserved the code, notebooks, sweep artifacts, and backlog notes
- excluded weights, outputs, and local clutter
- reframed the project as a lessons-learned archive for future reference
